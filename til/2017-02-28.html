Chez Scheme 2017-02-28

This is the first post from TIL series, where I’m going to capture my discoveries. They would be a kind of short references to either my progress or wonderful things encountered.

And today’s one is to Chez Scheme. It’s one of the fastest industrial Scheme implementations over there. And Racket is going to be rewritten on top of it —see Matthew Flatt’s announce =)

I’m going to keep an eye on it, perhaps one day I’ll use it for either

  • high-performance scripting
  • live coding

I dabbled with it today just a little, mostly trying FFI. Didn’t complete with libsoundio front page example yet, but documentation is great and wrapping mechanism is both thin and versatile.

I imagine Chez (or Racket built on top of it) to be a good foundation for live coding environment. While Extempore’s approach to have C-like xtlang is reasonable, I think that having optimizing Scheme compiler with efficient GC and GC/memory control should be enough soft-realtime-wise. We could trade maintaining own compiler for full-blown standard-compliant mature Scheme one.
